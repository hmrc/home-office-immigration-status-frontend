Accessibility statement for the Check Immigration Status service

This accessibility statement explains how accessible this service is, what to do if you have difficulty using it, and how to report accessibility problems with the service.

This page only contains information about the Check Immigration Status service, available at https://admin.tax.service.gov.uk/check-immigration-status

Using the service

You can use the Check Immigration Status service to view a customer's immigration status, as given by the Home Office. You can also view whether the customer has no recourse to public funds, their immigration route, and details on their previous nationality and immigration statuses from the past six months.

The Check Immigration Status service is run by HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC). We want as many people as possible to be able to use this service. For example, this means that you should be able to:

  • change colours, contrast levels and fonts
  • zoom in up to 400% without the text spilling off the screen
  • get from the start of the service to the end using just a keyboard
  • get from the start of the service to the end using voice recognition software
  • listen to the service with a screen reader (including most recent versions of JAWS, NVDA and Narrator)

We've also made the text in the service as simple as possible to understand.

AbilityNet has advice on making your device easier to use if you have a disability.

How accessible the service is

This service is fully compliant with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.1 AA standard.

Accessibility problems:

  • Missing third party libraries will mean that the ‘Search by National Insurance number’ page will not display correctly on different browsers.
  • When users are taken back to the ‘search by National Insurance number’ screen to change a customer’s details, the keyboard focus is placed on the field that users want to change on the ‘match has not been found’ page. Focus placement that is not predictable can confuse and disorient screen reader and screen magnification users.
  • People who rely on high contrast mode on Windows machines will not be able to identify the Government logos on the top and bottom of the pages.
  • For people who rely on screen magnification on iOS devices the HMRC logo is clipped and not readable fully.

Reporting accessibility problems

We are always looking to improve the accessibility of this service. If you find any problems that are not listed on this page or think we are not meeting accessibility requirements, report the accessibility problem.

For general IT issues, please contact the IT Helpdesk:

  • Go to IT helpdesk chat to chat online with one of our advisers (we'll email you a full transcript of the chat afterwards); or
  • Report non-urgent issues directly through Service Central or
  • Call us on 03003 300 003 to speak to an adviser; or
  • Book an appointment for an adviser to call you at a time that's convenient for you

If you are not happy with our response

The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (the 'accessibility regulations'). If you are not happy with how we respond to your complaint, contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS), or the Equality Commission for Northern Ireland (ECNI) if you live in Northern Ireland.

Technical information about this service's accessibility

HMRC is committed to making this service accessible, in accordance with the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018.
